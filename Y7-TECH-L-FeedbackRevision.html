What Makes a Complete Sentence?

These are real answers from our class. Which ones are complete sentences?

✗ NOT COMPLETE SENTENCES

"A phone stand"
"cardboard"
"the phone fall"
"not at all"

✓ COMPLETE SENTENCES

"I can see a phone stand made from cardboard."
"The phone fell because the base was too narrow."
"Cardboard is a lightweight, recyclable material."

A complete sentence needs:

Subject (who/what) + Verb (does what) + Detail (about what)

The phone stand   is made from   folded cardboard.

→

"its not that bad"

4 words · vague · no reason

→

AFTER

"The design is effective because it holds the phone at a comfortable angle."

13 words · specific · gives a reason with "because"

BEFORE

"both of them are good because they have good structure"

10 words · repeats "good" · not specific

→

AFTER

"Both designs use strong bases, but Design B held more weight because its wide base made it more stable."

18 words · specific · compound sentence with "but" + "because"

Grammar Quick Fixes

Common mistakes from our class — and how to fix them

Always capitalise "I"

✗ "i see a phone"

✓ "I can see a phone stand."

Past tense for what happened

✗ "the phone fall"

✓ "The phone fell."

✗ "We hid to make"

✓ "We had to make."

Don't skip small words

✗ "10 pieces paper"

✓ "10 pieces of paper"

✗ "are towers evaluate"

✓ "our towers evaluate"

The magic word: "because"

Short: "The design is good."
Better: "The design is effective because the wide base makes it stable."

Add "because" + a reason = instant improvement!

Swap everyday → technical words

"good" → effective, functional, suitable
"strong" → durable, rigid, reinforced
"looks nice" → aesthetic, appealing
"green" → sustainable, eco-friendly

TEEL Paragraph Structure

Here's a real TEEL paragraph from our class — one of the best examples:

T

Topic Sentence — states the main idea

"The design process is a useful framework for solving problems in Technology."

E

Evidence — gives a specific example

"For example, when we built paper boats, we followed the steps of investigate, design, produce, test, and evaluate."

E

Explanation — explains WHY the evidence matters

"This structured approach helped us identify problems early and improve our designs before the final build."

L

Link — connects back to the topic

"Therefore, the design process ensures that students create better products by thinking carefully at each stage."

Notice: each section starts with a signal word — "For example", "This", "Therefore" — these guide the reader.

TEEL — What to Avoid

✗ JUST COPYING THE SCAFFOLD

Plan Topic: "main key facts"
Plan Evidence: "An example"
Plan Explanation: "why the evidence matters"
Plan Link: "the connection to the topic"

This repeats the instructions, not YOUR ideas!

✓ USING YOUR OWN IDEAS

Plan Topic: "My tower design is strong and stable"
Plan Evidence: "Used cardboard with reinforced corners; tested with weights"
Plan Explanation: "The materials stop it from tipping or bending"
Plan Link: "My design shows careful planning and effective construction"

⚠ Does this sound like a Year 7 student?

"The cardboard phone stand presents itself as a very simple yet functional design. At first glance, you see an A-frame structure... The 60-degree angle is a thoughtful ergonomic choice, allowing users to watch videos comfortably." (90 words)

Compare with the same student's diagnostic: "In technology this term we have built columns of different shapes to figure out which shape is the strongest." (24 words, simple)

Your writing should sound like YOU. I'd rather read 2 honest sentences than a perfect paragraph that isn't yours.
