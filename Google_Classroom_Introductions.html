13T1-L13: Orthogonal (Multi-View) DrawingWeek 7

Last lesson we learned isometric drawing — showing 3D objects from an angled viewpoint.

Today we learn orthogonal drawing, also called multi-view or third-angle projection. Instead of one 3D view, you draw three separate 2D views: front, side and top. Engineers use orthogonal drawings because they show exact dimensions. You will practise converting between isometric and orthogonal views.

18T1-L18: Bridge Construction — Building Your TrussesWeek 9

Last lesson we wrote our design briefs, researched real bridges, and created three design sketches.

Today we BUILD! This is a practical construction lesson. You will use craft sticks and PVA glue to build your two side trusses. Important reminders: build FLAT on the desk (never in the air), use LAP JOINTS with at least 1 cm overlap, apply thin and even glue to BOTH surfaces, and clamp every joint. Your two trusses must be identical. Bring your design sketch — you will need it!
