🎯 What Is This Lesson?

Students work through an interactive academic writing program on their laptops. It teaches Technology vocabulary, text types, how to read exam questions, and how to write structured paragraphs (TEEL). The tool is self-paced — students click through modules and complete activities at their own speed. All work saves automatically in the browser.

Good news: You don't need any Technology subject knowledge. The tool teaches everything. Your job is to get students on task and keep them working.

📋 What to Say to Students (Script)

1 Opening (read aloud):
"Today you're working on an online writing program called Write It Right. It teaches you how to use Technology vocabulary and write better answers for assessments. Open your browsers and type the link on the board. Enter your full name at the top — your teacher will see your work."
2 After diagnostic (5 min in):
"Once you've done the word match and quiz, click 'See My Results'. The program will show you which level you're starting at — that's totally fine, it just adjusts the help you get. Then click through to Module 1 and keep working."
3 During work time:
"Work at your own pace. Read the instructions on each page. If you see a coloured help box, that's a hint for you. You don't have to rush — quality of writing matters more than speed."
4 About vocabulary:
"You'll see some words you haven't learned yet, like 'isometric' or 'orthogonal'. That's OK — the program explains what they mean. You'll cover them properly in later lessons."

🔧 Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
"The link doesn't work"Check the URL is typed exactly right. Try Google Chrome if using another browser.
"It says I'm Beginning level — is that bad?"No! Tell them: "It just means you get extra help boxes. That's a good thing."
"I lost my work"Work saves automatically IF they use the same browser on the same device. If they switched devices, they start fresh — that's OK.
"I finished everything!"Congratulate them. They can click the Print button at the top to save their work as a PDF, or revisit any module to improve their answers.
"I don't know what to write"Tell them to read the coloured help boxes and sentence starters. These give them a starting point.
Student is off-task / on another siteRedirect them back to the Write It Right URL. It's a single-page tool, so you can see the blue header bar when they're on the right page.
No internet / device issuePair them with a neighbour to share a screen, or give them a textbook to read quietly.

📊 What to Look For (Walking Around)

✅ On Track

  • Blue header bar visible on screen
  • Student name entered at top
  • Progress bar showing 1+ modules
  • Student typing in text boxes
  • Clicking through quiz options

⚠️ Needs Attention

  • Blank screen / different website
  • No name entered
  • Stuck on the same page for 10+ min
  • Copying from a neighbour
  • Typing random text to skip ahead

📌 Important Notes

AI Policy: Students must NOT use ChatGPT or any AI tool to write their answers. This is an academic integrity requirement. If you see a student using AI, ask them to close it and write in their own words.
Do NOT: Reset anyone's progress (the reset button is at the top — if a student accidentally clicks it, their work is gone). If asked, say "Check with your teacher next lesson."

✅ End-of-Lesson Checklist

  • All devices returned and plugged in to charge
  • Room is tidy (chairs pushed in, no rubbish)
  • Note any students who were absent or had device issues
  • Leave a note for the regular teacher: how far most students got (e.g., "Most reached Module 3")
